# Scoring Evaluation - Conversation 06 (Software Installation Issue)

## Conversation Summary
Customer experiencing software installation error. Agent provides no real solution, dismisses the problem, and suggests the customer contact someone else.

---

## Dimension Scores

### 1. Professionalism and Courtesy: **2/5** (Needs Improvement)
**Justification:** Agent starts unprofessionally ("Support. What's the problem?" - no greeting or name). Throughout the conversation, agent is dismissive ("I don't know what to tell you"), defensive when questioned ("Look, I'm just telling you what I see"), and lacks basic courtesy. Tone is curt and unhelpful.

### 2. Problem Resolution: **1/5** (Poor)
**Justification:** No resolution provided whatsoever. Agent admits not knowing the error code, makes random suggestions without conviction, and ultimately tells customer to contact computer manufacturer for a software issue. Offers refund instead of solving the problem. Customer's issue remains completely unresolved.

### 3. Empathy and Understanding: **1/5** (Poor)
**Justification:** Zero empathy demonstrated. Agent shows no understanding of customer's frustration, suggests the problem "might be on your end" without investigation, and dismisses customer's valid concerns. When customer expresses frustration about paying for non-working software, agent's response is to suggest a refund rather than fix the issue.

### 4. Communication Clarity: **2/5** (Needs Improvement)
**Justification:** Communication is vague and uncertain throughout ("That's weird", "I'm not sure then", "I don't know what to tell you"). Agent admits inability to look up error codes and provides unclear, unhelpful suggestions. No clear explanation of what the error means or definitive troubleshooting steps.

### 5. Efficiency and Effectiveness: **1/5** (Poor)
**Justification:** Extremely inefficient. Agent asks basic questions that should have been checked first, wastes time "looking up" information without finding answers, provides suggestions the customer already tried, and makes no progress toward resolution. Ends by deflecting to supervisor without attempting to schedule callback or take ownership.

---

## Overall Score: **7/25**

**Rating Category:** Poor - Significant improvements required

### Key Weaknesses:
- No product knowledge (doesn't know error codes for own software)
- Defensive and dismissive attitude
- Zero problem-solving ability
- Blames customer's equipment without investigation
- Refuses to take ownership
- No escalation path provided (supervisor "might" be available later)

### Critical Failures:
- **No resolution attempted** - Agent gave up immediately
- **Lack of resources** - Couldn't look up error codes in own system
- **Inappropriate deflection** - Suggested customer contact computer manufacturer for software issue
- **Poor customer retention** - Suggested refund instead of solving problem
- **No follow-up plan** - Left customer with no clear next steps

### Impact on Customer:
- Customer paid for software that doesn't work
- No solution provided after full conversation
- Customer feels dismissed and frustrated
- No timeline for resolution
- Likely to request refund and leave negative review

### What Should Have Happened:
1. Agent should have access to error code documentation
2. Escalate to technical specialist or supervisor immediately
3. Collect diagnostic information (system specs, logs)
4. Create support ticket with clear follow-up timeline
5. Show empathy and take ownership
6. Provide temporary workaround if possible
